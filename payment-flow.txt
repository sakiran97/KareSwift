Because your platform allows offline negotiation between the customer and the technician (e.g., the technician opens the device, finds a deeper issue, and they agree on a new price of ₹1500 instead of ₹500), the payment flow needs to be flexible.

Here is a breakdown of how this works in the real world (like Urban Company), why an in-app wallet is still highly recommended, and my suggestions for your scenario:

1. How it works in real-time
In a real-world scenario with Razorpay or Stripe:

The customer tops up their wallet using UPI, Credit Card, or Netbanking.
When the repair is done, the technician enters the Final Negotiated Amount into their app.
The customer's app instantly updates to show the final bill.
The customer clicks "Pay", and the money securely moves from the Customer's Wallet to the Platform's Escrow account (and eventually to the Technician).
2. Is an in-app payment system required?
Strictly speaking, No. You can allow the customer to simply pay the technician directly via Cash or personal Google Pay/PhonePe when the job is done.

However, relying solely on offline payments creates a massive problem for you as the platform owner: How do you collect your commission? If a technician makes ₹2000 in cash, they have no incentive to come back and pay your platform the 15% commission fee.

3. My Recommended Scenario (The Hybrid Approach)
I highly recommend keeping the Wallet system, but adjusting the workflow to a Hybrid Model:

Pre-paid Diagnosis Fee: To prevent fake bookings and compensate the technician for travel, require the customer to pay a small, non-refundable booking fee (e.g., ₹99) from their Wallet before the technician is dispatched.
Final Payment Options: When the repair is complete and the final price is negotiated, the technician enters the final amount in their app. The customer's app then gives them two choices:
Pay via Wallet (Secure, tracked, generates automatic invoice)
Pay via Cash / Direct UPI to Tech (Convenient for the customer)
The Technician Wallet (For Commissions): This is the secret to making offline marketplaces work! If the customer pays in Cash, your platform automatically deducts your 15% commission from the Technician's Wallet. If the technician's wallet balance drops below ₹0, the app blocks them from accepting new orders until they top-up their wallet and pay you what they owe.
